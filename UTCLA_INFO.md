*"Our ancient Tjukurpa (Law/Lore) is the governing authority for UTCLA and underpins all aspects of our society, governance, daily lives and self determination. The Men's and Women's Councils of the UTCLA collectively decide over matters concerning every man women and child of the their respective Countries. All decision concerning the health and well being of all members rest with the Tribal Councils. ## 1. WHO WE ARE

The United Tribal Countries Land Alliance (UTCLA) is a voluntary alliance of self-governing Tribal Nations across Terra Australis.

The Alliance exists to:

- Strengthen cultural governance
- Coordinate collective decision-making
- Protect Our collective land, Water, Airways and cultural authority
- Facilitate responsible economic cooperation
- Support intergenerational sustainability

UTCLA does not replace the sovereignty of any Tribal Nation.
Each Nation retains full authority over its own Country and internal affairs.

---

## 2. CULTURAL FOUNDATION

### Tjukurpa – Supreme Cultural Authority

The foundation of the Alliance is Tjukurpa (Law/Lore) — the ancient governance system derived from nature and upheld through ceremony, kinship, songlines, and custodial responsibility.

Tjukurpa governs:

- Cultural obligations
- Custodial authority
- Inter-Nation relationships
- Responsibilities to Country
- Ethical decision-making

Tjukurpa is the unifying cultural framework of the Alliance.

---

## 3. LAWFUL POSITION

### Autonomous Self-Determination

Indigenous peoples are recognised under international law as having the right to self-determination, including the right to:

- Determine internal governance structures
- Maintain customary law systems
- Manage cultural and community affairs
- Participate in economic development

These principles are affirmed in the International Humanitarian Law article (Customary Law)

- United Nations Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples

and reflected in the

- International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights
- International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights

The UTCLA operates within this framework of recognised internal self-determination.

The Alliance does not assert external statehood, but affirms cultural sovereignty, governance legitimacy, and collective cooperation consistent with international norms.

## 4. MEMBER COUNTRIES / TRIBAL NATIONS

Founding Member Countries/Nations include:

- **Pintupi Wati Tjuta Tjungarinyi** (Central Western Desert)
- **Ni'ripono Gwata Tu Sovereign Government** (Cape York region)
- **Majagi** (Larrakia, Tiwi Island and Brinkin peoples)

Additional Countries/Tribal Nations across multiple regions have entered into formal Treaty partnerships with the Alliance.

Each member Country/Nation remains autonomous and self-governing.

---

## 5. GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

### Representation

Each Member Country/Nation appoints:

- One Male Representative
- One Female Representative

Representatives speak exclusively for their own Country/Nation.

No Country/Nation may make decisions for another Country/Nation or internal affairs.

### Decision-Making Levels

**Local Authority**

Each Tribal Council governs:

- Community wellbeing
- Cultural matters
- Land stewardship
- Internal governance

**Collective Authority**

Matters affecting multiple Countries/Nations are addressed collectively through consensus-based deliberation guided by Tjukurpa.

All collective decisions:

- Require consultation
- Are transparently tabled
- Respect equal representation
- Uphold cultural authority

## 6. LAW & POLICY FRAMEWORK

Alliance policies are grounded in:

- Tjukurpa (Cultural Law)
- Applicable domestic legal frameworks
- Relevant principles of international and trade law where appropriate

Dispute resolution mechanisms may utilise appropriate legal forums depending on the nature of the matter.

The Alliance is progressively formalising policies relating to:

- Cultural Protection
- Land and Resource Stewardship
- Economic Cooperation
- Infrastructure Collaboration
- Diplomacy Protocols
- Strategic Investment

- Joint development initiatives

Economic activity is guided by:

## 9. CULTURAL AUTHORITY & INTEGRITY

Cultural legitimacy is the foundation of governance.

The Alliance governance process:

- Requires matters to be tabled before representatives
- Encourages open deliberation
- Prioritises consensus
- Reduces corruption risk
- Ensures collective transparency

Authority flows from cultural custodianship — not from imposed structures.

---

## 10. STRATEGIC OBJECTIVES

**Unity** — Strengthen cooperation between self-governing Countries and Tribal Nations.

**Cultural Continuity** — Preserve language, ceremony, kinship, and Law.

**Healing & Community Wellbeing** — Support holistic development across Countries/Nations.

**Sustainable Development** — Advance responsible land-based economic systems.

**Equality & Fairness** — Ensure inclusive representation and balanced governance.

**Innovation** — Adopt appropriate technologies that support cultural and environmental sustainability.

---

## 11. RELATIONSHIP TO MEMBER CONSTITUTIONS

The UTCLA is a collective private members alliance.

Each Member Country/Nation maintains its own:

- Constitution
- Governance framework
- Cultural authority structures

The Alliance does not replace individual Nation constitutions but coordinates collective matters between them.
